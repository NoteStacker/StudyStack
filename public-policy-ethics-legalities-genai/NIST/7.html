The Three Pillars of GOVERN

1. Ownership — Accountability and Sign-Off

What it means: Someone must be personally accountable for AI outcomes. Sign-off authority must be established before deployment. Accountability cannot be diffused across teams or delegated to "the algorithm."

Key Questions: Who is accountable? Who signs off? Who is responsible when things go wrong?

Executive Actions: Appoint named owners (CAIRO, CRO, Business Unit Head); require executive sign-off for high-risk AI; tie accountability to performance reviews.

2. Policies — Rules and Boundaries

What it means: Define what AI can and cannot do. Establish clear rules for deployment. Create boundaries that teams must operate within.

Key Questions: What AI use cases are prohibited? What are mandatory testing requirements? What documentation is required?

Executive Actions: Create AI acceptable use policy; define prohibited applications; establish mandatory pre-deployment requirements.

3. Risk Appetite — Thresholds and Tolerances

What it means: Define how much AI risk the organization is willing to accept. Set quantitative thresholds for error rates, bias, and performance. Establish when to stop, intervene, or roll back.

Key Questions: What is acceptable error rate? What disparity ratio is tolerable? When must a model be pulled?

Executive Actions: Define acceptable bias thresholds; set performance thresholds; establish escalation triggers.

The Boardroom Lens: Governance Hierarchy

LEVEL 1 · OVERSIGHT

Board of Directors

Ultimate Accountability — Fiduciary responsibility; cannot delegate accountability.

LEVEL 2 · STRATEGY

C-Suite & AI Governance Council

Executive Accountability — Implements Board strategy; reports upward.

LEVEL 3 · EXECUTION

AI Development, Data & Ops Teams

Technical Accountability — Builds and operates within boundaries.

Strategy flows down. Governance sets direction; teams execute.
Accountability flows up. Leadership owns outcomes; cannot delegate.
Ultimate Accountability rests with the Board.

1. Why must the Board own ultimate AI risk?
Answer: Fiduciary duty, cannot delegate accountability, risk appetite authority, regulatory expectations, stakeholder accountability.
2. Describe the three-tier governance hierarchy.
Answer: Board (Oversight) → C-Suite (Strategy) → Teams (Execution). Strategy flows down; accountability flows up.
3. Explain the three pillars of GOVERN.
Answer: Ownership (accountability), Policies (boundaries), Risk Appetite (thresholds). Together they form the foundation of AI governance.
4. What does "strategy flows down, accountability flows up" mean?
Answer: Leadership sets direction; teams execute. But ultimate responsibility rests with leadership, not technical teams.
5. What happens when GOVERN is weak or absent?
Answer: No accountability, no boundaries, no thresholds. MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE cannot function. Risks become invisible.
