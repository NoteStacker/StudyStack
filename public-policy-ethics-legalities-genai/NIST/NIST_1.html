The AI Incident Scenario
NIST AI RMF | Exam Ready Notes

Scenario: Day 3 after deployment — AI system made a wrong decision affecting 10,000 customers.

Three Simultaneous Crises

📰 Media Crisis

Headlines scream “Biased AI Denies Loans to Minorities” — brand reputation destroyed in hours.

⚖️ Regulatory Crisis

Regulator demands model documentation, audit logs, and may freeze operations.

🏃 Customer Crisis

Trust evaporates; competitors launch “human-first” campaigns instantly.

Core Lesson: AI failures are governance failures — caused by gaps in accountability, testing, risk appetite, and incident response.
🔹 NIST Connection: GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE functions would have prevented or mitigated this crisis.
“What happens next?” — The question that forces organizations to prepare before Day 1.
1. Explain the three crises that unfold when an AI system fails at scale. Why is this considered a governance failure rather than a technical failure?
Answer: The three crises: Media (viral reputation damage), Regulatory (inquiries + audit demands), Customer (trust erosion & competitor attacks). It is a governance failure because root causes are unclear accountability, no defined risk appetite, missing pre-deployment testing, absent documentation, and no incident plan — all governance responsibilities, not coding bugs.
2. What documentation do regulators demand after an AI failure? Why is this documentation critical?
Answer: Model development records, training data sources, testing/validation results, full audit logs, explainability docs, and pre-deployment risk assessments.
Critical because it proves responsible AI practices; without it, organizations face fines, operational freezes, and legal liability.
3. How do the four NIST AI RMF functions (GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE) apply to the Day 3 scenario?
Answer:
GOVERN → assign clear accountability for post-deployment monitoring.
MAP → identify proxy discrimination risks (zip code) before launch.
MEASURE → detect disparate impact using fairness metrics pre-deployment.
MANAGE → incident protocols (rollback, kill-switch, comms) for rapid containment.
4. What is the difference between reputational risk and regulatory risk in AI failures? Provide examples.
Answer: Reputational risk → damage to brand trust (viral headlines, competitor "human-first" campaigns). Regulatory risk → legal penalties, compliance violations, operational freezes (regulator notice, audit demands). Both strike simultaneously and require different response strategies.
5. Why is the question “What happens next?” critical for organizations deploying AI? What should they prepare before Day 1?
Answer: It forces acknowledgment that AI failure is inevitable, not hypothetical. Before Day 1, organizations must prepare: crisis comms protocols, model documentation, defined risk appetite, named accountability owners, incident response plans, and bias/security testing. Preparation turns chaos into controlled response.
